Privacy Policy

Last updated: November 24, 2025

Introduction

Welcome to SnowMyScreen. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This privacy policy explains how we handle your information when you use our macOS application.

Information We Collect

SnowMyScreen is designed with privacy in mind. The app operates entirely on your local device and does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information to external servers.

Data Storage

All settings and preferences you configure in SnowMyScreen are stored locally on your device. This includes:

  • Snowfall customization settings
  • Application preferences
  • Display configurations

This data remains on your device and is not shared with us or any third parties.

Third-Party Services

SnowMyScreen does not integrate with any third-party services or analytics platforms. Your usage of the app is completely private.

macOS Permissions

SnowMyScreen may request the following macOS permissions to function properly:

  • Screen Recording permission - to display snowfall effects on your screen
  • Accessibility permission - to overlay effects on your desktop

These permissions are used solely for the app's core functionality and no data is collected or transmitted.

Updates to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date.

Contact Us

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or SnowMyScreen, please contact us through our website.

Your Rights

Since we do not collect any personal data, there is no data to access, modify, or delete from our servers. All your data remains on your device, and you can delete it at any time by uninstalling the application or resetting its preferences through macOS System Settings.

Children's Privacy

SnowMyScreen does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. The app is designed to be privacy-friendly for users of all ages.

Data Security

Since all data is stored locally on your device, the security of your settings depends on your device's security measures. We recommend keeping your macOS up to date and following Apple's security best practices.
